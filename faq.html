Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to the most common questions about Vertex Security.

How do I set up Vertex Security?

Setting up Vertex Security is quick and easy. After you've invited the bot to your server and granted it the necessary administrator permissions, simply run the /setup moderate command. The bot will then automatically create a moderation log channel, a private owner log channel, and configure essential security settings for you.

What are the different security levels?

Vertex offers three distinct security levels to fit your community's needs:

  • Relaxed: Minimal filtering and raid protection, ideal for private or very small communities.
  • Moderate: A balanced approach with robust filtering and standard anti-raid measures. This is the recommended setting for most communities.
  • Strict: Maximum security with highly sensitive filters and a low threshold for anti-raid activation. Best for public servers with a high risk of malicious activity.
You can change your security level at any time with the /security-level command.

Does it work with non-English servers?

Yes, absolutely! Our advanced content filter is built to be multilingual and can detect toxic content, slurs, and spam in over 50 different languages simultaneously. This ensures that no matter where your members are from, your server remains a safe and welcoming place.

Is Vertex Security really free?

Yes, and it will always be free. Every single feature—from the advanced AI anti-raid system to the multilingual filter and private logs—is available at no cost. There are no premium plans, hidden fees, or feature limitations. Our bot is open-source and built for the community, by the community.

How does the anti-raid system work?

The anti-raid system monitors the rate of new members joining. When it detects a large influx (e.g., 5 or more users joining in under 30 seconds), it automatically activates a lockdown. New members are then required to complete a CAPTCHA verification to gain access, effectively stopping bot raids and mass-join attacks without disrupting your existing members.

Can banned users appeal their punishment?

Yes, our system is designed for a fair process. If a user is banned, they can DM the bot with the !appeal command. This will prompt them to fill out a modal form. The completed appeal is then sent directly to a designated moderation channel in your server, allowing your staff to review their case.

What are private owner logs?

Private owner logs are a separate, dedicated log channel that is only visible to the server owner and other highly trusted staff members. This channel records every critical moderation action, such as bans, unbans, and bot configuration changes, providing a secure and tamper-proof record for complete accountability.
